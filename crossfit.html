Dynamic cross-fit training is the key to reaching your next goal. Far too many hockey players go through high school only concerned with bulking up. As a result, they end up doing one dimensional, football-lineman-type workouts. Cross-fit training is a combination of high tempo strength work mixed with cardiovascular work to build endurance. This amalgam of muscle building and coordination building has become the predominant focus of trainers at both the professional and collegiate levels. Exercises that simulate playing the game — whether that's stick handling, keeping your head up, working on peripheral vision, or practicing your stride — which are an invaluable part of preparation that, unfortunately, is often overlooked.

A combination of free weights, weighted vests, sled pulls, and bosu ball balance-work are essential for high school players looking to improve. Explosiveness — a function of the aforementioned recipe — is one of the most important things in hockey; those first couple strides taken while accelerating differentiate the good hockey players from the great ones.
